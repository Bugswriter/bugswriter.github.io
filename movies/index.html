On this page, you will find the cinema of my choice. my cherry-picked films collection

I find beauty in cinematographic shots and evocative scores. At just two hours, I believe films are too brief for complex storylines or deep character development. Therefore, I seek aesthetic visuals and immersive atmospheres over intricate plots.

I typically avoid mainstream action, thrillers, or comedies. My preference leans towards thoughtful dramas. Many find my favored films slow, even "boring." When recommending from my list, I usually start with accessible stories, then gradually introduce others to the more meditative, melodic tones I enjoy. For me, it's a peaceful, almost meditative experience.

I dislike violence or anything unnatural in films. I'm drawn to settings rich in nature, village life, or ancient vibes, though urban backdrops can sometimes appeal.

My viewing habits lean heavily into Asian cinemaâ€”Japanese, Chinese, and Korean films are my staple. Indian films, despite my location, rarely offer the novelty I seek.

I've sometimes considered filmmaking, having studied camera work and related elements. The rise of AI could potentially simplify this process, making it a future possibility.

While I check Rotten Tomatoes for credibility, I generally avoid deep dives into directorial specifics or technical analyses. Such discussions often diminish my personal viewing experience; I prefer to simply watch, appreciate, and move on without excessive attachment.

All my films are downloaded and organized within my Jellyfin library. My entry into Korean cinema, and indeed my passion for it, began with my favorite actress: Jun Ji-hyun.

Hana and Alice (2004)

This Japanese coming-of-age film follows two best friends, Hana and Alice, as they navigate their quirky high school lives and a shared crush on a senpai. The movie explores themes of **friendship, first love, youthful innocence, and the sometimes elaborate fictions we create**.

Il Mare (2000)

A South Korean romantic drama where two people living two years apart in time communicate through a magical mailbox at a seaside house called "Il Mare." The film delves into themes of **love across time, destiny, loneliness, and the poignant beauty of waiting**.

Kaili Blues (2015)

This Chinese art-house film follows a doctor in Guizhou province as he searches for his nephew, leading him on a dreamlike journey into a mysterious town where past, present, and future seem to intertwine. Its themes include **memory, time, regret, family bonds, and the mystical elements of ordinary life**.

Little Forest (2018)

A heartwarming South Korean film about a young woman who leaves her challenging city life to return to her quiet rural hometown, where she finds solace in nature and cooking. The movie beautifully explores themes of **self-sufficiency, the healing power of nature, finding contentment in simplicity, and reconnecting with one's roots**.

Maborosi (1995)

Directed by Hirokazu Kore-eda, this Japanese film follows a young woman haunted by the inexplicable death of her first husband and her attempts to find peace in a new marriage. It's a meditative and poignant exploration of **grief, loss, memory, the persistence of sorrow, and the search for acceptance**.

My Sassy Girl (2001)

This iconic South Korean romantic comedy follows the tumultuous and hilarious relationship between a timid college student and a spirited, unpredictable girl. Its themes include **unconventional love, fate, growing up, and challenging gender stereotypes in romance**.

Shoplifters (2018)

Another masterpiece by Hirokazu Kore-eda, this Japanese drama portrays a family of small-time criminals who rely on shoplifting to survive, and their bond with a young girl they take in. It deeply explores themes of **family (chosen vs. biological), poverty, morality, social judgment, and the complexities of human connection**.

Not One Less (1999)

A Chinese drama directed by Zhang Yimou, telling the story of a 13-year-old substitute teacher in a rural village who is determined not to lose a single student. When one boy leaves for the city, she embarks on a journey to bring him back, exploring themes of education, perseverance, poverty, and social inequality in modernizing China.

On the Beach at Night Alone (2017)

A South Korean drama by Hong Sang-soo, following an actress reflecting on love and loss after an affair with a married man. Set between Germany and Gangneung, the film explores themes of longing, solitude, self-awareness, and the emotional aftermath of romantic relationships.
